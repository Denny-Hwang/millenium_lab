## 3. Pattern 1 — recurring blocker: Boolean rigidity in algebraic moduli

**Observation.** The single most repeated blocker is that *Boolean
circuits form a discrete (0-dimensional) locus inside any algebraic
moduli*. It appears in three escalating forms:

- A005 / G009: the naive discrete reading of L02 makes $H^1 = 0$
  identically.
- A006: even after the Grassmannian-product construction supplies a
  positive-dimensional *ambient*, the Boolean-evaluation locus is
  0-dimensional inside it (verified explicitly at $(2,3,2)$).
- A007 / A008 / A009: every coherent sheaf supported on (or built
  from) the Boolean locus either trivializes, lives in the wrong
  cohomological degree, or detects realized-ness with the *inverted*
  biconditional direction.

**Diagnosis.** This is not a sequence of independent bugs. It is one
structural fact — *Boolean rigidity* — observed from four angles.
Boolean circuits are combinatorial objects; embedding them in an
algebraic moduli does not give them algebraic deformations, so the
cohomology that the natural-proofs reformulation (B-002) needs is
simply not present on the Boolean locus.

**Recommendation.** Treat Boolean rigidity as a *named obstruction*
for the program, not as a series of patchable gaps. Any future
sheaf-cohomology approach to AC⁰ lower bounds must address it at the
outset. This is the central lesson of A005–A009 and should be
recorded in `bridges/B-002` and `docs/primer/field-primers/03-p-vs-np.md`.

## 4. Pattern 2 — converging insight: the cohomological degree must match ambient dimension

**Observation.** A002 L05 fixed the invariant as $H^1$. A008 showed
that in a 2-dimensional ambient the right non-vanishing Ext degree is
$\mathrm{Ext}^2$, not $\mathrm{Ext}^1$; A009 confirmed the
$\mathrm{Ext}^2$ class is the natural one at T3. The "correct" degree
is dimension-dependent.

**Diagnosis.** A002's $H^1$ choice was an unexamined default,
appropriate only for 1-dimensional ambients. The program implicitly
assumed a fixed cohomological degree across all ambient dimensions —
an error that took until A008 to surface.

**Recommendation.** Any restatement of L05 (whether in a revived (R1)
or in the (R2) pivot) must carry the cohomological degree as a
*parameter* tied to ambient dimension, not as a constant.

## 8. What a future reader should take away

The (R1) sheaf-cohomology attack on AC⁰ lower bounds, pursued
carefully across five technical attempts (A005–A009), failed for a
single structural reason: **Boolean circuits are rigid in algebraic
moduli, so the cohomology the natural-proofs reformulation needs is
absent on the Boolean locus.** The failure is informative — it tells
the next investigator not to expect $H^*$ of a coherent sheaf on a
Boolean-circuit moduli to detect non-AC⁰-membership, and to either
(a) work on a positive-dimensional arithmetic moduli (the GCT / (R2)
route) or (b) find a genuinely non-coherent invariant. This is
exactly the kind of negative knowledge charter §4.4 ("failure as
asset") is meant to preserve.
