# 01 — Poincaré Conjecture (reference only; solved 2003)

> Solved by Perelman (2002–2003). Retained as a primer file because
> the conjecture and its solution are routinely cited in this
> repository's discussions of *what a Millennium-class solution
> looks like* and *what verification at L5–L7 entails in practice*.

---

## 1. Problem statement

TODO: The original statement: every simply connected, closed
3-manifold is homeomorphic to the 3-sphere $S^3$. Paraphrase
carefully — the Generalized Poincaré Conjecture in dimensions
$\geq 5$ was settled by Smale in 1961, and the 4-dimensional case
(topological) by Freedman in 1982.

## 2. Why it is hard (was)

TODO: Dimension 3 sits between low-dimensional geometric topology
and high-dimensional surgical topology. Tools available in higher
dimensions (general position, Whitney trick) fail; tools in
dimension 2 (uniformization) do not directly generalize.
