## 1. Problem statement

TODO: For any compact simple gauge group $G$, prove the existence
of a non-trivial quantum Yang–Mills theory on $\mathbb{R}^4$ with
a mass gap $\Delta > 0$. The official CMI statement requires both
the construction (Wightman axioms or equivalent) and the gap.

## 2. Why it is hard

TODO: Quantization of a non-abelian gauge theory on continuous
spacetime has no established mathematical foundation; the
perturbative theory works as a heuristic but is not a proof of
existence. The mass gap is the gap between vacuum and the
lowest-energy single-particle state and is observed
experimentally and on the lattice but not derived.

## 4. Prior results to know about

TODO:

- Lattice formulation (Wilson 1974): rigorous on a finite
  lattice; the continuum limit is the hard part.
- Constructive QFT successes in lower dimensions:
  $\phi^4_2, \phi^4_3, Y_2, Y_3$ — but not $Y_4$.
- Asymptotic freedom (Gross–Wilczek, Politzer 1973):
  perturbative evidence the theory should exist.

## 5. Failed approaches and barriers

TODO: Direct rigorous construction at the continuum has resisted
all techniques to date. Reflection positivity establishes part
of the framework but not the full set of axioms with mass gap.
